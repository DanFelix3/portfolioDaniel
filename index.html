— Who I am

About Me

From a young age, I was always curious about how games and digital experiences were built. That curiosity gradually became the reason I chose Computer Science Engineering — not just because of technology itself, but because of the creativity and problem-solving behind it. As I started learning programming, I became more interested in the design and logic of how things work. Internships helped me to explore more about buidling applications that required both logical thinking as well as designing user experiences that lit the fire even more.

Over the past three years, I have also worked as a designer at the college level, handling design requirements across multiple departments and events. That experience helped me understand not only design consistency and visual communication, but also the importance of collaboration, adaptability, and creating work that serves a purpose beyond aesthetics.
